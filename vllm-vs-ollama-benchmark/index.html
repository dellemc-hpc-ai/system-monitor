Fair Comparison Key Findings (Qwen 0.5B)

MISMATCHED SCALEThis 0.5B model is far too small to stress L4. Both engines saturate bandwidth easily — Ollama appears faster but this may not hold at 8B+ scale.

STABLEvLLM has near-zero variance across all phases (496ms ± 3.7ms steady). Ollama is also stable (448ms ± 21ms steady) once initialized.

INIT OVERHEADOllama's first-ever request takes 3,867ms (model initialization). Subsequent requests are ~380ms. vLLM has no such penalty after container start.

NEXT STEPNeed a 7B GGUF test for meaningful results. Qwen2.5-7B or Llama-3.1-8B in GGUF format loaded by both engines.

Cache provides zero speedup — prompt is too short

Cold and Cache Hit phases are nearly identical for all engines. The ~35-token prompt KV is dwarfed by the 256-token generation. Cache shines when system prompts are long (RAG, multi-turn) — here it doesn't matter.

TurboQuant has a steep steady-state cliff — 109% slower under burst

TurboQuant starts fast (5.2s) but degrades to 10.8s under burst. fp8 is stable at 10.9s across all phases. For multi-user production servers, fp8 is the safer choice.

Findings (Llama 3.1 8B, different formats per engine)

  • TurboQuant ~2x faster cold-start: 5.2s vs 10.9s (fp8) vs 51s (Ollama). KV compression (2.5x ratio) reduces memory bandwidth pressure.
  • fp8 is the stable workhorse: 10.9s cold → 11.4s steady (4% degradation). Predictable, no cliff.
  • Ollama is stable but 5x slower than TurboQuant: 51-52s across all phases. 73% lower memory is its main advantage.
  • ⚠ Don't compare engines across formats: AWQ INT4 vs Q4_K_M GGUF are different quantizations. See the fair comparison (Test 3) for same-GGUF results.
